Next, you’re clearing buildings with a team lead you’ve never talked to before today. You follow him up the staircase of the hotel. He declares the building has been cleared. Across the street, the Medic is attending to a casualty.

Armastock is about getting together, seeing how other ArmA 3 groups conduct their sessions, and giving your group the opportunity to undergo a 50+ player count scenario. The event is held every once every 2 months, and hosted by different groups on a rotation schedule. Armastock usually runs anywhere from 4 hours to 8 hours, and once lasted for roughly 12 hours. Cooperative and PvP scenarios are played during the event, with cooperatives being the main event. The co-op missions for Armastock are centered around large infantry maneuvers, used in combination with armored and/or air support assets. PvP scenarios are usually played after cooperatives, so stick around for those. Also, a theme is sometimes implemented into Armastock, such as a ‘spooky mission’ for Halloween, for example. The event is used as an exhibition for attending groups to get hands on experience with mods they don't normally use, such as ACRE or TFAR. Finally, and above all else, Armastock is all about the experience. You could command an M1A2 Abrams with someone you might not know within the hills of Chernarus, be a part of a company of infantry as they assault a strategic airfield on the island of Malden, and revolt against the UN ‘Peacekeepers’ as a ragtag militia in post-war Zargabad, all in a single day of play.
